Our Approach

Roofing Built for Louisiana, By Louisiana

Louisiana's climate demands more from a roof than almost anywhere else in the country. Extreme UV exposure, humidity above 80% for months at a time, tropical storms, and hail all conspire against roofing materials that aren't designed for our conditions.

We only install roofing systems from manufacturers that understand Gulf Coast weather — with wind ratings, algae resistance, and thermal performance specs appropriate for our region. And every installation is backed by both a manufacturer product warranty and our own workmanship guarantee.

  • Manufacturer-approved installation on every job
  • Full cleanup and haul-away included

FORTIFIED Systems
Best Protection

IBHS FORTIFIED™ Roofing

The FORTIFIED designation requires specific materials, installation methods, and third-party verification. It's the highest standard for wind and hail resistance — and it qualifies you for major insurance discounts.

  • Class 4 impact rated

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a roof replacement take?
Most residential roof replacements in Louisiana are completed in 1–2 days. Larger or more complex roofs may take up to 3 days. We work efficiently to minimize disruption to your household.
Will my homeowner's insurance cover a new roof?
If your roof was damaged by a storm event (hurricane, hail, wind), your insurance likely covers all or most of the cost. We help you document damage and work with your adjuster to maximize your claim. Roofs damaged by age or wear are typically not covered.
What is the best roofing material for South Louisiana?
For most homeowners, we recommend either high-quality architectural asphalt shingles with algae resistance for value, or metal roofing for longevity. If insurance premiums are a concern, upgrading to a FORTIFIED system can pay for itself through premium savings over time.
Do you offer a workmanship warranty?
Yes. Every Cypress Gulf Coast installation includes a workmanship warranty in addition to the manufacturer's product warranty. We stand behind our work — if there's a problem with the installation, we fix it at no cost to you.
How do I know if I need a full replacement or just a repair?
Generally, if your roof is under 15 years old and the damage is isolated, a repair may suffice. If it's over 20 years old or has widespread damage, replacement is often more cost-effective long-term. Our free inspection gives you an honest assessment — we'll never push a replacement if a repair is the right call.
