Types of Damage

We Handle Every Type of Storm Damage

Hurricane & Wind Damage

High winds from tropical systems and thunderstorms can peel shingles, blow off ridge caps, and expose decking. We respond fast to prevent further interior water damage.

Hail Damage

Hail causes bruising and granule loss that weakens shingles over time. Our inspectors know exactly what to look for and how to document it for your insurance claim.

Flooding & Water Intrusion

Standing water and roof leaks can cause catastrophic interior damage. We identify the source, stop active leaks, and restore your roof's waterproofing system.

After a Storm

Warning Signs Your Roof Needs Immediate Attention

Many homeowners underestimate storm damage because it's not always visible from the ground. These are the red flags that mean you need a professional inspection right away:

Missing or curled shingles
Visible granules in gutters
Ceiling water stains or drips
Sagging or bowed decking
Damaged or missing flashing
Dented gutters or vents
Musty smell in attic
Cracked chimney caps

Insurance Claims

We Handle Your Claim — Start to Finish

Navigating an insurance claim is stressful. Our team acts as your advocate throughout the entire process, making sure you receive the full settlement you're entitled to.

1

Free Roof Inspection & Documentation

We thoroughly inspect your roof and document all damage with photos, measurements, and a written report — the same format insurers expect.

2

File Your Insurance Claim

Contact your insurance company to open a claim. We'll help you understand what to report and what not to say that could reduce your payout.

3

Meet the Adjuster — With Us Present

We meet your adjuster on-site to walk through all documented damage. Our expertise ensures nothing gets overlooked or undervalued.

4

Receive Your Settlement

Once your claim is approved, we begin work immediately. In most cases, your out-of-pocket cost is only your deductible.

5

New Roof Installed & Certified

We complete your installation, provide all warranty documents, and notify your insurer — giving you a fully restored, warranted roof.
